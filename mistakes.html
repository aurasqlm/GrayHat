Safe vs Unsafe

Compare common user behaviors and learn what to avoid.

Scenario: Suspicious Email Attachment

✓ SAFE

Scan & Verify

You notice the sender is unknown. You hover over the link, see it's weird, and delete the email immediately. You scan your PC just in case.

✕ UNSAFE

Curiosity Click

"Invoice_FINAL.pdf.exe"? Sounds important! You download and open it. Ransomware instantly encrypts your files.

Scenario: Coffee Shop Wi-Fi

✓ SAFE

VPN Encrypted

You connect to "Free Coffee WiFi" but immediately turn on your VPN. Your traffic is encrypted and invisible to sniffers.

✕ UNSAFE

Exposed Login

You log into your bank account on open Wi-Fi. An attacker on the same network intercepts your session cookie.

Scenario: Creating an Account

✓ SAFE

Unique & Random

You use a password manager to generating a 24-char string. You enable 2FA with an authenticator app.

✕ UNSAFE

Recycled Password

You use "Fido123" because it's your password for everything. When Adobe gets hacked, your bank is next.

More Security Pitfalls

Unsafe

Reusing Passwords

Using "Password123" across all sites creates a domino effect.

Fix: Unique Passwords + Manager
Unsafe

Skipping Updates

Running outdated software with known CVEs.

Fix: Auto-Updates ON
Unsafe

Ignoring 2FA

Relying solely on passwords is unsafe.

Fix: Enable TOTP/YubiKey
Unsafe

Clicking SMS Links

Smishing attacks are rising. "Delivery Failed" links are traps.

Fix: Verify via Official App
Unsafe

Found USB Drives

Plugging in random USBs leads to BadUSB attacks.

Fix: Destroy/Report it
Unsafe

Public Wi-Fi Banking

Logging into banks on unencrypted cafe Wi-Fi.

Fix: Use VPN
Unsafe

Leaving Devices Unlocked

Walking away from your laptop at a coffee shop.

Fix: Win+L / Lock Screen
Unsafe

Posting Travel Plans

Announcing you are away from home on public social media.

Fix: Post after returning
Unsafe

Default Router Passwords

Leaving "admin/admin" on your home router.

Fix: Change immediately
