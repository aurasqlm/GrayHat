Fundamental

Creating an Unbreakable Password

1

Use Length, Not Complexity

A phrase like Horse-Battery-Staple-Correct is easier to remember and harder to crack than P@55w0rd.

2

Never Reuse Passwords

If one site gets hacked, hackers will try that password everywhere. Unique passwords for every account prevent this chain reaction.

3

Use a Password Manager

Humans are bad at randomness. Let a secure software generate and store your 20-character passwords.

Email Security

Identifiying Phishing Attacks

1. Check the Sender Address

Does the email come from support@paypal-security-check.com instead of service@paypal.com? It's a scam.

2. Look for Urgency

"Act now or your account will be deleted!" Scammers use fear to make you click without thinking.

3. Hover Before You Click

Hover your mouse over links. If the URL looks suspicious or uses a URL shortener, do not click.
